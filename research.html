01 · Research

Research interests

Current

  • Long-read sequencing. Information-theoretic quality validation of Oxford Nanopore (ONT) squiggle data; identifying when raw signal carries enough information for confident basecalling.
  • Mathematical methods for sequencing. Signal-processing and statistical-inference tools for nanopore data, including spacekime-style re-parameterizations and interpolation methods for low-coverage regions.
  • Differential privacy. Privacy-preserving mechanisms for longitudinal biomedical signals that preserve clinically meaningful structure.
  • Linguistics. Historical Germanic linguistics and information-theoretic approaches to language structure (M-local entropy, locality of information).

Past

  • Psychiatric genomics. Statistical modeling of the Prechter Bipolar and Substance Use Disorder cohorts; mixed-model phenotyping of mood trajectories (concluded 2025).
  • Synthetic morphogenesis. Autonomous biobots assembled from adult human somatic progenitor cells, with the Levin Lab at Tufts.
  • Bioelectricity. Meta-analysis of bioelectric measurements across cancer, embryogenesis, and regeneration.
  • Developmental biology. Zelda binding-site dynamics in the bicoid gene of Drosophila, with the Garcia Lab at UC Berkeley.

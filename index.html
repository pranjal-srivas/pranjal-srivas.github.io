About

Pranjal Srivastava

Ph.D. Candidate, Bioinformatics · Dual MA, Linguistics · University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

My current research is on Oxford Nanopore long-read sequencing and the mathematical methods that go with it: information-theoretic quality validation of squiggle data, differential privacy for longitudinal biomedical signals, and signal-processing approaches to basecalling.

I work in the Athey & Dinov Labs at U-M. Earlier, I led the analysis on the Prechter Bipolar and Substance Use Disorder cohorts (concluded 2025), which produced the LAMP mixed-model phenotyper.

I came to bioinformatics from a triple-major B.A. at UC Berkeley in Molecular & Cell Biology (Genetics), Statistics, and Linguistics. I have ongoing collaborations on autonomous biobots with the Levin Lab at Tufts, and a standing interest in historical and information-theoretic linguistics.
